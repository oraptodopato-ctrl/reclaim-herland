About

The project

The problem

Despite the growing discourse on gender within architecture and landscape architecture, the work of women landscape architects in Portugal remains critically underexplored. This gap in the historiography stems from several interconnected factors: limited access to archives, the systematic undervaluing of women's contributions, and the near-absence of in-depth research on the topic.

Preliminary research reveals a striking paradox: women participated in landmark modernist projects — including the urban plans for Chelas, Olivais, and Sines — yet their names remain largely absent from public memory and historical accounts.

What we do

Reclaim.HERland recovers, reinterprets, and disseminates the narratives of pioneering women who helped shape Portugal's modernist landscapes between the 1940s and 1980s. The project focuses on six landscape architects whose professional trajectories have been documented through archival research and oral history.

Drawing on Elizabeth Meyer's (1997) framework of "recovery" and "critique," the team identifies biases, exclusions, and omissions in existing archival materials — reconstructing biographies, tracing contributions to public spaces, and revealing new perspectives on gender dynamics within the profession.

Methodology

The project combines three complementary approaches:

  • Archival research — systematic survey of institutional, personal, and professional archives, including provenance analysis. All descriptions are managed in AtoM (Access to Memory), following ICA standards (ISAD(G), ISAAR(CPF)).
  • Oral history — twelve semi-structured interviews with landscape architects and their contemporaries, using an ethnographic approach. Given the advanced age of many participants, this work carries particular urgency.
  • Critical analysis — feminist historiography and discourse analysis to evaluate archival materials, identify systemic biases, and revise historiographical narratives.
